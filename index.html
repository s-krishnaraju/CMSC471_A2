Passengers Enplaned Across U.S. Airports in 2024

Filter Airports by Passengers Enplaned

Write Up

Introduction

This dataset is from the Bureau of Transport Statistics and shows the number of passengers that each U.S. airport enplanned (boarded onto a plane) in the year of 2024.

Design Decisions

The main visualizations are a Scatter Map and a Pie Chart. The Scatter Map shows the location of each airport and info about the airport on hover (name, location, and passenger enplanement), as well as having the ability to pan and zoom. The size of the scatter point of each airplane is scaled based on that airport's passenger enplanements and I also added a color scale that utilizes passenger enplanements ( top 10 airports have unique colors while the rest use a color scale based on shades of blue). I felt this was a good design decision since it allows a viewer to see which airports have high passenger enplanements at a glance. The unique colors of the top 10 airports also match up with the Pie Chart which helps link the two visualizations together. Finally, I added a slider which can filter the airports based on passengers enplaned (both Pie Chart and Scatter Map). Since the dataset has a lot of airports that have a neglible amount of enplanements, it helps the viewer filter out the noise. It could also help them draw some additional insights from the data by looking at airports within a specific range of passenger enplanements.

Data Resources

Data was from Bureau of Transport (Source). I took some inspiration from the Bureau of Transport's visualization on the dataset for my Scatter Map but I added additional functionality to the map and linked it with another visualization, a Pie Chart.

Development Process

I did the project solo, so none of the work was split. The development process was pretty straightforward as I already have experience using Plotly in an internship I did. I would say one aspect that was slightly challenging was deciding on how to color the scatter points. I made the map first with the color scale feature and then the pie chart. However I realized I needed to link the two charts together colorwise but I didn't want to get rid of the color scale feature on the map. I ended up striking a middle ground and just giving the top 10 airports on the map the pie chart color and the rest of the airports the color scale. I would say I spent about 3 hours on the project.
